Tonjiru
- Shinya shokudo -

Description

Tonjiru is a Japanese soup made with pork and vegetables, flavoured with miso. It is a more substantial version of miso soup, with a larger quantity and variety of ingredients.

Shinya Shokudo is a popular japanese drama which features stories about japanese food and people. Shinya means "late Night" and shokudo means "diner" so this drama is about people from all walks of life, townspeople, visitors, and all kinds of people who gather at this diner. These stories will fill your stomach and your heart.

Ingredients

  • Sliced pork
  • Japanese white radish
  • Carrots
  • Shitake mushrooms
  • Burdock
  • Konjac
  • Green onion
  • White miso
  • Red miso
  • Dashi
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Cut japanese white radish and carrots and burdock and shitake up into small pieces and tear konjac into bite size pieces.
  2. Saute pork with vegetable oil until the meat changes color.
  3. Add the ingredients that you cut up in step1
  4. Once these ingredients are coated with oil then add dashi soup.
  5. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  6. Add miso a bit at a time.
  7. Serve with green onion.
